Connor S. Kenaston is a doctoral candidate at the University of Virginia. His teaching and scholarship examine the history of religion, politics, and culture in the United States. He is also interested in anti-racist pedagogy and digital history. His dissertation, “Faith Networks: National Broadcasting and the Making of American Religion,” explores religion and mass culture during the supposed “Golden Age” of radio.
