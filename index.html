Dr. Connor S. Kenaston is the Assistant Professor of History at Randolph College. His scholarship focuses on religion, social movements, and the intersections of capitalism and culture in the twentieth-century United States. He teaches broadly on topics within US and Atlantic history, as well as public history. Connor received his PhD from the University of Virginia in 2022. His scholarship has been featured in publications such as Modern American History,” Hybrid Pedagogy and Methodist Review. He is currently working on a history of religious broadcasting, tentatively titled “The Big Three: Commercial Radio Networks and the Making of Tri-Faith America.”
