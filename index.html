Dr. Connor S. Kenaston is the Assistant Professor of History at Randolph College. His teaching and scholarship examine the social and cultural history of US and the Atlantic World. He specializes in American religious history and the intersections between culture and capitalism. Connor received his PhD from the University of Virginia in 2022. His scholarship has been featured in publications such as _Modern American History_,” [_Hybrid Pedagogy_](https://hybridpedagogy.org/with-care-and-context/),” and [_Methodist Review_](https://methodistreview.org/index.php/mr/article/view/131).” He is currently working on a history of religious broadcasting, tentatively titled “The Big Three: Commercial Radio Networks and the Making of Tri-Faith America.”
